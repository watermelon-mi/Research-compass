Specific Instructions:

🧭 Research Compass

Discover how you think before choosing what to research.

Research Compass is a self-discovery tool designed to help students explore their research preferences, understand different research environments, and make more informed decisions about undergraduate research opportunities.

Rather than telling users what they should do, Research Compass encourages reflection, exploration, and curiosity.

⸻

🌱 Why Research Compass?

Many students begin exploring research without knowing:

* What kind of research they actually enjoy
* Whether they prefer experimental or computational work
* How different research environments operate
* What questions they should ask before joining a lab

Research Compass was built to bridge that gap.

The goal is not to recommend a specific professor.

The goal is to help students better understand themselves.

⸻

✨ Features

🔬 Research Style Assessment

Complete a series of scenario-based questions designed to explore how you approach research.

The assessment evaluates five dimensions:

Dimension	       Description
Experimentation	   Preference for hands-on investigation and practical work
Data Analysis	   Interest in patterns, datasets, and quantitative reasoning
Theory Building	   Interest in mechanisms, concepts, and explanations
Project Tempo	   Preference for short-term feedback or long-term exploration
Human Interaction  Preference for people-focused versus system-focused work

Unlike traditional personality tests, questions are designed around realistic research situations and daily academic experiences.

⸻

🧠 Research Archetypes

Based on your responses, Research Compass generates a research archetype.

Examples include:

* The Mechanism Seeker
* The Explorer
* The Analyst
* The Builder
* The Collaborator

Each archetype includes:

* Core motivations
* Strengths
* Potential challenges
* Preferred research environments
* Reflection questions

The goal is not categorization, but self-understanding.

⸻

📊 Research Profile Visualization

Your assessment results are displayed through:

* Radar charts
* Progress bars
* Dimension summaries

These visualizations help users quickly understand their research tendencies and preferences.

⸻

🌍 Research Field Explorer

Based on your profile, Research Compass suggests fields that you may enjoy exploring.

Potential recommendations include:

* Genetics
* Molecular Biology
* Neuroscience
* Psychology
* Public Health
* Bioinformatics
* Computational Biology
* Data Science
* Biomedical Engineering
* Ecology
* Systems Biology

Each field includes:

* Overview
* Why it may appeal to you
* Common activities
* Typical research questions
* Example day-in-the-life scenarios

Recommendations are exploratory rather than prescriptive.

⸻

📅 Day In The Life

Research can look very different depending on the field.

Research Compass provides example daily schedules to help students better understand what research work may actually involve.

Examples include:

* Computational Biology
* Neuroscience
* Public Health
* Genetics
* Psychology

These examples are educational illustrations rather than exact representations.

⸻

🏛️ Lab Compatibility Analyzer

Users can paste:

* Professor biographies
* Lab descriptions
* Research statements
* Faculty profiles

Research Compass analyzes the content and compares it with the user’s research profile.

The system generates:

* Overall compatibility score
* Areas of alignment
* Potential challenges
* Reflection prompts

No AI services are required.

The analysis is performed locally using a rule-based keyword matching system.

⸻

🌿 Research Environment Reflection

Finding a good research environment is often more important than finding the “perfect” topic.

This section helps students evaluate research environments through three categories:

Green Flags To Look For

Examples:

* Mentorship opportunities
* Skill development
* Collaborative culture
* Clear project ownership

Potential Challenges To Consider

Examples:

* Long project timelines
* High independence requirements
* Technical learning curves
* Heavy experimental workloads

Questions You Might Ask

Examples:

* How often do students meet with the PI?
* What training is provided?
* What does a typical week look like?
* How are projects assigned?

The purpose is to help students ask better questions, not make decisions for them.

⸻

✍️ Reflection Summary

At the end of the experience, Research Compass generates a personalized reflection summary that connects assessment results, research interests, and compatibility insights.

The summary is designed to encourage thoughtful exploration rather than provide definitive answers.

⸻

🎯 Design Philosophy

Research Compass is built around several principles:

Exploration over prediction

The platform helps users discover possibilities rather than prescribing paths.

Reflection over ranking

The platform emphasizes self-understanding rather than scores.

Curiosity over certainty

Research is inherently uncertain. The tool embraces that uncertainty.

Education over recommendation

The goal is not to tell users where to go.

The goal is to help users ask better questions.
